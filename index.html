About Matt


Matt Thauvette is a professional drummer who has been performing and teaching a wide range of musical styles since 2002. He currently tours internationally with ECMA, MNS and SOCAN award winner Mo Kenney as well as east coast favorite, Adam Baldwin. Along with Baldwin’s debut record, Matt’s discography also includes records by Young River and the ECMA nominated, instrumental-afro-soul group, The Chronos Band.


Before moving to Halifax, Matt focused his studies on jazz and improvised music, attending The Banff Center for the Arts and earning an Honours Degree and Diploma in Jazz Performance from St Francis Xavier University and Grant MacEwan College.


While his touring schedule keeps him busy, Matt loves teaching music to all ages and levels of aspiring musicians. He is currently accepting students for private lessons. Please see the lesson information below and feel free to contact Matt with any questions regarding lessons.

Lessons


Over the past 10 years Matt has been working to develop a teaching and lesson plan for all ages and levels of drummers. Drum lessons are tailored to each student’s musical needs and tastes.


Lessons cover the fundamentals of reading music, proper technique, how to play with other musicians and explore creative thinking. While development is the goal, having fun is always the focus.

Materials


The majority of the lesson material will be giving to students during their lessons, however students will also work extensively from Ted Reed’s legendary book, Progressive Steps to Syncopation for the Modern Drummer. Discounted copies of the book are available to purchase at lessons.


Unique Opportunities


  • Every student has the chance to be recorded during their drum lessons.


  • Videos of students playing during their lessons are often used for student development. They are also fun for younger students to share with family and friends.


  • While there are guidelines for learning, students are encouraged to bring in songs and material they want to learn and perform. Students also often enjoying preparing performances for school talent shows.


  • For students studying music in school, theory and keyboard lessons are available as well as tutoring for the schools’ music curriculums.
